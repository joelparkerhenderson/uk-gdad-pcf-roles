# Business analyst - upskill resources

This page focus on learning and growth resources for the work role of business analyst.

- The resources include links to online courses, blogs, research articles, videos, and books. 

- These help with job upskilling, work training, professional development, and career advancement,

- These are intended to be good starting points, not a comprehensive catalogue.

We welcome constructive feedback to improve this page and these lists.

- GitHub issues and/or pull requests are welcome.

- The resources are found via AI searches then go through human-in-the-loop proofing.

- For more contact Joel Henderson <joel@joelparkerhenderson.com>

## Coursera courses

- [IBM Business Analyst Professional Certificate](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/ibm-business-analyst-professional-certificate): This program helps you build essential skills, technical expertise, and hands-on experience with key business analysis tools. The program aligns with the Business Analysis Body of Knowledge (BABOK) to prepare you for completing the Entry Certificate in Business Analysis (ECBA). Covers requirements gathering, stakeholder management, and business analysis fundamentals.

- [Microsoft Business Analyst Professional Certificate](https://www.coursera.org/professional-certificates/microsoft-business-analyst): You can complete this in as little as three months and learn how to identify and analyze business problems, use Microsoft Excel for data analysis, and model business processes and data using industry-standard techniques. Ideal for those seeking practical skills with Microsoft tools.

- [Business Analysis Fundamentals](https://www.coursera.org/learn/business-analysis-fundamentals): This course provides foundational knowledge in business analysis, teaching learners to effectively bridge the gap between business needs and IT solutions. Covers core business analysis concepts, techniques, and best practices for facilitating better decision-making processes.

## Blog posts

- [Guide to Gather Requirements as a Business Analyst in 2025](https://techpulsion.com/how-to-gather-requirements-guide/): Business analysts rely on a variety of techniques, including: Interviews, Workshops, Surveys and Questionnaires, Observation and Job Shadowing, Prototyping and Wireframing, and Document Analysis. Comprehensive guide updated for 2025 with modern best practices.

- [How Business Analyst Gather Requirements: Driving Success from Day One](https://www.businessanalyststoolkit.com/how-business-analyst-gather-requirements/): Emphasizes that gathering requirements isn't simply about taking notes in meetings—it's about uncovering true business needs, managing conflicting priorities, and building strong relationships with stakeholders. Business analysts use a mix of techniques including interviews, workshops, observation, and document analysis.

- [Golden Rules of Stakeholder Engagement in Business Analysis](https://www.batimes.com/articles/golden-rules-of-stakeholder-engagement-in-business-analysis/): Best practices include working to become a BA stakeholders can depend on and building relationships based on trust. The article emphasizes maintaining regular communication and keeping stakeholders informed of project progress and BA deliverables.

- [5 Common BPA (Business Process Analysis) Techniques](https://www.blueprintsys.com/blog/5-bpa-business-process-analysis-techniques-you-should-know): Five common Business Process Analysis techniques include: Value Analysis (quantifies process value using metrics), Root Cause Analysis (identifies delays and bottlenecks), Gap Analysis (reveals when process duration is too long), Predictive/Simulation Analysis (tests process improvements), and Process Mapping and Flowcharting.

- [How to Write User Stories and Acceptance Criteria](https://www.thebagirl.com/user-story/): Writing requirements is a key skill for business analysts, and in agile environments, user stories often serve as the primary requirements. Covers the INVEST criteria and practical guidance for creating effective user stories with clear acceptance criteria.

- [Agile Business Analysis: Navigating the Modern Project Landscape](https://thebaguide.com/blog/agile-business-analysis-navigating-the-modern-project-landscape/): Agile Business Analysis has emerged as a crucial component in ensuring successful project outcomes. This blog explores agile business analysis methodologies, best practices, tools, and future trends. Published March 27, 2024.

- [Data Modelling: A Guide For Business Analysts](https://baexcellence.com/blogs/business-analysis-excellence-blog/data-modelling-a-guide-for-business-analysts): Data modeling is a critical skill in business analysis. The guide explains that data modeling begins with defining entities (items about which data will be collected), which can be anything tangible like people, places, or products.

- [Business Requirements Document: Tips & Templates](https://www.responsive.io/blog/write-business-requirements-document): Offers tips, templates, and real-world examples for creating clear and effective business requirements documents. A BRD is defined as a formal document that outlines a project including overview, goals, scope, key stakeholders, requirements, potential risks and budget.

- [Interviews: A Must-Have Elicitation Technique for Business Analysts](https://www.adaptiveus.com/blog/technique/interviews/): Interviews are a widely used and highly effective elicitation technique in business analysis, involving one-on-one conversations between the business analyst and stakeholders to gather information, clarify requirements, and understand organizational needs.

- [BPMN Tutorial: Learn Business Process Model and Notation](https://camunda.com/bpmn/): Camunda offers real-world examples and teaches how to use BPMN 2.0 diagrams to model processes and improve business-IT alignment. Business Process Model and Notation is the global standard for process modeling.

## Research articles

- [Business analysis and requirements engineering: The same, only different?](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/220428036_Business_analysis_and_requirements_engineering_The_same_only_different): This research article explores the business analyst role and compares it with requirements engineering, finding that while there is overlap, business analysis includes activities outside the scope of requirements engineering.

- [Stakeholder Engagement: Past, Present, and Future (2022)](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/00076503211066595): Comprehensive literature review examined 90 articles in leading academic journals focusing on stakeholder engagement. The researchers presented a descriptive analysis of stakeholder engagement research over a 15-year period, identifying the moral, strategic, and pragmatic components of stakeholder engagement.

- [Business Process Modeling- A Comparative Analysis](https://aisel.aisnet.org/jais/vol10/iss4/1/): By Jan Recker, Michael Rosemann, Marta Indulska, and Peter Green (2009). This paper comparatively assesses representational analyses of 12 popular process modeling techniques and explores representational root causes for shortcomings in process modeling practice.

- [Prioritization of Agile Requirement Elicitation Techniques Using Fuzzy Logic](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s40815-024-01920-y): A 2024-2025 study that proposed an approach using Fuzzy logic to prioritize agile requirements elicitation techniques. The study found that Use Case, User Story, Prototype, Scenario, and UML Diagram are widely implemented techniques.

- [Business Analysis Competency Model](https://www.iiba.org/professional-development/business-analysis-competency-model/): The Business Analysis Competency Model is an ongoing initiative of IIBA since 2010, based on research in organizations of various sizes and industries. It describes the knowledge, skills, abilities, and personal characteristics required to perform effectively as a business analysis professional, identifying 30 Business Analysis performance competencies.

- [Practical Applications of Systems Thinking to Business (2020)](https://www.mdpi.com/2079-8954/8/2/14): This paper summarizes research on Systems Thinking for business management and explores several examples of business failures due to a lack of Systems Thinking application. Systems thinking is essential for business analysts to understand complex organizational systems.

- [Mastering User Acceptance Testing (UAT) for Business Analysts](https://transforminguxwitharpit.medium.com/mastering-user-acceptance-testing-uat-for-business-analysts-a-practical-roadmap-5e68be3c15e9): UAT is a gatekeeping process in the software development lifecycle to ensure a new product is ready for public release. The business analyst plays a crucial role in planning, coordinating, and facilitating UAT activities.

- [Business analytics and decision science: A review of techniques in strategic business decision making](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/378580402_Business_analytics_and_decision_science_A_review_of_techniques_in_strategic_business_decision_making): Recent studies have shown companies who adopt "Data-Driven Decision Management" achieve significant productivity gains. Business analysis can deliver consistently on data quality and help businesses switch from being 'disrupted' to becoming 'disruptors'.

- [How enterprise architecture leads to organisational benefits](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0268401222000883): This 2022 article builds upon the dynamic capabilities view and argues that firms that developed their dynamic capabilities using enterprise architecture (EA) are better equipped to cope with change. Identifies three distinct mechanisms through which EA contributes to organisational benefits.

- [Role of Business Analysts on Digital Transformation](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=4763261): Academic research analyzing the role of business analysts in digital transformation. Business analysts play a crucial role in guiding companies through digital transformation, acting as the bridge between business and technical teams, ensuring digital initiatives align with organizational goals.
