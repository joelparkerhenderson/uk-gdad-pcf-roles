
# User Researcher - Learning and Professional Development Resources

This page focus on learning and growth resources for the work role of user researcher.

- The resources include links to online courses, blogs, research articles, videos, and books. 

- These help with job upskilling, work training, professional development, and career advancement,

- These are intended to be good starting points, not a comprehensive catalogue.

We welcome constructive feedback to improve this page and these lists.

- GitHub issues and/or pull requests are welcome.

- The resources are found via AI searches then go through human-in-the-loop proofing.

- For more contact Joel Henderson <joel@joelparkerhenderson.com>

## Coursera courses

- [Conduct UX Research and Test Early Concepts](https://www.coursera.org/learn/conduct-ux-research): Part of Google's UX Design Professional Certificate program, this course teaches how to plan and conduct usability studies to gather feedback about designs. Covers user research methods, conducting research sessions, and testing early concepts.

- [User Experience: Research & Prototyping](https://www.coursera.org/learn/user-research): Comprehensive course covering user experience research methods, creating detailed personas, and rapid prototyping. Teaches both qualitative and quantitative research techniques for understanding user needs and behaviors.

- [User Research and Design (University of Minnesota)](https://www.coursera.org/learn/design-research): Focuses on user research methodologies and design processes. Covers planning research studies, conducting user interviews, analyzing findings, and translating research insights into design decisions.

## MIT OpenCourseWare courses

- [Qualitative Research Methods: Conversational Interviewing (21A.819.1x)](https://mitxonline.mit.edu/courses/course-v1:MITxT+21A.819.1x/): Teaches how to construct interview protocols that guide interviewees through topics without explicitly bringing them up. Focuses on exploring user experiences without directing respondents toward particular answers.

- [Qualitative Research Methods: Data Coding and Analysis (21A.819.2x)](https://mitxonline.mit.edu/courses/course-v1:MITxT+21A.819.2x/): Covers what to do with qualitative data once collected, including transcribing data, creating codes and codebooks, coding data, analyzing codes, and making sense of analysis using existing and new theory.

- [Crafting Research Questions and Qualitative Methodology (11-233)](https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/11-233-crafting-research-questions-and-qualitative-methodology-fall-2005/): Topics include narrowing research interests, using quantitative and qualitative techniques complementarily, and addressing interviewing and fieldwork challenges in urban studies and planning contexts.

## Blog posts

- [9 Essential User Research Methods for 2025](https://formbricks.com/blog/user-research-methods): Practical roundup of nine essential research techniques published July 2, 2025. Provides actionable steps for user interviews, usability testing, surveys, A/B tests, and frameworks for when to use each method and common pitfalls to avoid.

- [How to conduct user research for (almost) free](https://blog.logrocket.com/ux-design/how-to-conduct-user-research-for-almost-free/): Published March 2025, features low- and no-cost research methods including desk research, reusing internal data, guerrilla testing and interviewing, lean user interviews, low-cost surveys, and community engagement strategies.

- [UX research methods: The complete guide for 2025](https://greatquestion.co/blog/ux-research-methods-guide): Explores ten common UX research methods including user interviews, focus groups, surveys, diary studies, field studies, card sorting, tree testing, usability testing, prototype testing, and A/B testing with guidance on when to use each.

- [Qualitative UX Research: Complete Guide for Beginners [2025]](https://careerfoundry.com/en/blog/ux-design/qualitative-ux-research/): Covers four primary qualitative methods: user interviews, focus groups, shadow sessions, and diary studies. Explains why qualitative research needs only 5-8 participants and reveals emotionally-driven data useful for stakeholders.

- [UX Research Synthesis Methods for Actionable Insights](https://www.looppanel.com/blog/ux-research-synthesis): Discusses multiple synthesis methods including affinity diagramming, journey mapping, thematic analysis, persona creation, and empathy mapping. Provides practical techniques for transforming research data into actionable insights.

- [How to Conduct Inclusive UX Research [Complete Guide]](https://careerfoundry.com/en/blog/ux-design/inclusive-ux-research/): Explains why inclusion in user research matters and how it lays a strong foundation for designing experiences that are accessible, welcoming, and safe for everyone. Covers recruiting diverse participants and inclusive research practices.

- [UXR Relationships 101: Stakeholder Management](https://sprig.com/blog/user-research-stakeholder-management): Defines stakeholders as anyone who impacts your work or whom you can impact. Suggests using research skills to uncover stakeholder needs and pain points, building effective working relationships through empathy.

- [3 Types of stakeholders in UX research: Managing expectations and gaining trust](https://uxinsight.org/3-types-of-stakeholders-in-ux-research-managing-expectations-and-gaining-trust/): Identifies three stakeholder types: the Novice, the Enthusiast, and the Skeptic. Provides strategies for managing each type's expectations, which is the single most important factor for ensuring UX research project success.

- [Making user research work in agile sprints](https://medium.com/melon-experience-design/making-user-research-work-in-agile-sprints-5af658db551f): Illustrates how research activities fit into a 10-day sprint. Emphasizes getting everyone on the team involved in research activities such as attending research sessions and participating in analysis.

- [How to conduct user research in an agile environment](https://dovetail.com/blog/user-research-agile/): Recommends aiming for one major strategic study per quarter that occurs across all sprints. Suggests timeboxing work into similar chunks within sprints, with specific time allocations for recruitment, planning, and conducting research.

## Research articles

- [User Experience Methods in Research and Practice](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/10.1177/00472816211044499): Published in Journal of Technical Writing and Communication. Charts current UX methods through systematic literature review of publications from 2016-2018 and survey of 52 practitioners. Findings indicate contemporary UX research favors mixed methods, with usability testing especially popular. Candice Lanius, Ryan Weber, Joy Robinson (2021).

- [Exploring principles of user-centered agile software development: A literature review](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0950584915000129): ScienceDirect 2015 paper characterizing software development through agile approaches for velocity and flexibility, combined with user-centered design. Examines hybrid models called user-centered agile software development (UCASD).

- [Improving Agile Software Development using User-Centered Design and Lean Startup](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0950584921001701): ScienceDirect 2021 study noting limitations in agile methods for problem understanding. Conducted nine-month case study with two industry teams combining agile with User-Centered Design and Lean Startup discovery-oriented approaches.

- [Planning Qualitative Research: Design and Decision Making for New Researchers](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/1609406920967174): SAGE journals publication addressing alignment among research questions, data collection, and analysis. Provides practical guidance for new researchers planning qualitative studies. Lesley Eleanor Tomaszewski, Jill Zarestky, Elsa Gonzalez (2020).

- [Universal design, inclusive design, accessible design, design for all](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/271657803_Universal_design_inclusive_design_accessible_design_design_for_all_different_concepts-one_goal): Explores different accessibility concepts and their historical, methodological, and philosophical aspects. Discusses how these approaches focus on increasing interactive system accessibility for the widest possible range of users.

- [Designing Personas with Empathy Map](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/276207468_Designing_Personas_with_Empathy_Map): Explores how personas, an HCI technique for gathering user information, can be integrated with empathy mapping. Demonstrates practical application for Human Computer Interaction design processes.

- [Using Empathy Mapping in Design Thinking Process for Personas Discovering](https://link.springer.com/chapter/10.1007/978-3-030-67101-3_15): Springer 2021 paper revisiting the persona concept and drawing connections for using empathy maps to build personas within design thinking processes. Applicable to HCI design and marketing analysis.

- [Ethnographic research in healthcare - patients and service users as participants](https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/32202439/): PubMed discussion paper providing introduction and overview of ethnography as qualitative research methodology. Outlines usefulness in understanding patient and service user experiences during healthcare journeys.

- [A survey of the use of ethnographic methods in the study of libraries and library users](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0740818812000096): ScienceDirect 2012 paper identifying 81 studies with five main types of ethnographic research methods: observation, interviews, fieldwork, focus groups, and cultural probes.

- [Contextual Inquiry: Inspire Design by Observing and Interviewing Users in Their Context](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/contextual-inquiry/): Nielsen Norman Group article on contextual inquiry as ethnographic field study involving in-depth observation and interviews. Defines four principles: Context, Partnership, Interpretation, and Focus for understanding work practices.

## Videos

- [What Is User Research? (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/what-is-user-research/): Foundational video from NN/g explaining the basics of user research, its importance in the design process, and how it helps create user-centered products and services.

- [When to Use Which UX Research Method (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/when-use-which-ux-research-method/): 5-minute video helping determine the most effective UX research method based on five criteria to meet research goals. Maps 20 methods across three dimensions within typical product development processes.

- [Usability Testing 101 (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/usability-testing-101/): Overview of this popular observational methodology to uncover problems and opportunities in designs. Covers planning, conducting, and analyzing usability tests effectively.

- [5 Qualitative Research Methods (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/articles/which-ux-research-methods/): Overview of key qualitative methods beyond standard usability testing, especially useful for early discovery studies. Covers interviews, field studies, diary studies, and ethnographic research.

- [Thematic Analysis of Qualitative User Research Data (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/): Covers how to summarize and make actionable qualitative research data through thematic analysis. Teaches techniques for identifying patterns and themes across research sessions.

- [Card Sorting: Why & When (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/card-sorting-why-when/): Explains card sorting methodology for understanding user mental models and information architecture. Covers when to use open vs. closed card sorting and how to analyze results.

- [Card Sorting & Tree Testing = Best Friends (UXtweak)](https://blog.uxtweak.com/card-sorting-tree-testing-best-friends/): YouTube video demonstrating how card sorting and tree testing work together. Shows how card sorting generates IA ideas while tree testing evaluates IA effectiveness.

- [Card Sorting with UXtweak: Step-by-Step Guide](https://www.uxtweak.com/videos/card-sorting-step-by-step/): Step-by-step video showing how to set up a card sorting study, including choosing between open, closed, or hybrid card sorting approaches based on research goals.

- [User Panels: Recruit Faster, Research Smarter (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/user-panels/): Covers strategies for building and maintaining user panels for ongoing research. Explains benefits of having ready access to research participants and best practices for panel management.

- [Collecting UX Metrics During Qualitative User Studies (Nielsen Norman Group)](https://www.nngroup.com/videos/collecting-ux-metrics-qualitative-user-studies/): Teaches how to collect quantitative metrics during qualitative research sessions. Covers task success rates, time on task, and satisfaction ratings to complement qualitative observations.

## Books

- [Just Enough Research (Second Edition)](https://www.amazon.com/Just-Enough-Research-Erika-Hall-ebook/dp/B0CW2WCJM6): Distills user research experience into a brief cookbook of research methods. 2024 Second Edition features brand-new chapter on surveys with updated examples throughout, guiding web professionals on ethics and effects of research. Erika Hall.

- [Observing the User Experience: A Practitioner's Guide to User Research (Second Edition)](https://www.amazon.com/Observing-User-Experience-Practitioners-Research-ebook/dp/B008QWEH62): Comprehensive guide with in-depth coverage of 13 user experience research techniques. Second edition adds entirely new material on observational research, mobile usability, diary studies, remote research, and cross-cultural projects. Elizabeth Goodman, Mike Kuniavsky, Andrea Moed.

- [Usability Testing Essentials: Ready, Set...Test! (Second Edition)](https://www.amazon.com/Usability-Testing-Essentials-Ready-Test-ebook/dp/B08CR945SM): Practical, step-by-step approach to planning and conducting usability tests. Covers analyzing and applying results, handling budgetary and time restrictions, and international usability testing considerations. Carol M. Barnum.

- [Interviewing Users: How to Uncover Compelling Insights (Second Edition)](https://www.amazon.com/Interviewing-Users-Uncover-Compelling-Insights-ebook/dp/B0CHBH8W92): Invaluable interviewing techniques and tools for conducting informative interviews. Shows how to move from simply gathering data to uncovering powerful insights about people, dispelling the myth that interviewing is trivial. Steve Portigal.

- [The User Experience Team of One: A Research and Design Survival Guide (Second Edition)](https://www.amazon.com/User-Experience-Team-One-Research-ebook/dp/B0D2ZBT485): Essential guide for solo UX practitioners. Updated second edition with loads of new material from the past 10 years, emphasizing down-to-earth approaches that deliver big impact over time-consuming techniques. Leah Buley.

- [Sprint: How to Solve Big Problems and Test New Ideas in Just Five Days](https://www.amazon.com/Sprint-Solve-Problems-Test-Ideas-ebook/dp/B010MH1DAQ): New York Times bestseller presenting unique five-day process for solving tough problems. Created at Google by Jake Knapp, proven at over 100 companies. Integrates human-centered design, prototyping, and rapid user testing. Jake Knapp, John Zeratsky, Braden Kowitz.

- [Thinking with Data: How to Turn Information into Insights](https://www.amazon.com/Thinking-Data-Turn-Information-Insights-ebook/dp/B00HX91CGM): Practical guide showing how to put the why before the how through analytical skills. Introduces the CoNVO scoping framework. Teaches techniques for turning data into actionable knowledge for product decisions. Max Shron.

- [Research Practice: Perspectives from UX researchers in a changing field](https://www.amazon.com/Research-Practice-Perspectives-researchers-changing-ebook/dp/B08P9VZJFN): Captures day-to-day of research practice including working with peers and stakeholders, raising awareness of research, making tradeoffs, and building larger teams. Focuses on stories of people who study people. Gregg Bernstein.

- [Quantifying the User Experience: Practical Statistics for User Research (Second Edition)](https://www.amazon.com/Quantifying-User-Experience-Practical-Statistics-ebook/dp/B01IIXZVU4): Practical guide on using statistics to solve common quantitative problems in user research. Addresses questions about competitive usability, task completion rates, and statistical significance of findings. Jeff Sauro, James R. Lewis.

- [2024-2025 Newbies Guide to UI/UX Design Using Figma](https://www.amazon.ca/2024-Fundamentals-Experience-User-centric-Interactive-ebook/dp/B0D6PJFSMC): Comprehensive guide covering fundamentals of UI and UX design including user research. Specifically labeled for 2024-2025 timeframe with focus on creating user-centric interfaces and interactive prototypes using Figma. Anthony E. Sanchez.
