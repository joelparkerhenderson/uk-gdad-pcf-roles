
# Product manager - upskill resources

This page focus on learning and growth resources for the work role of product manager.

- The resources include links to online courses, blogs, research articles, videos, and books. 

- These help with job upskilling, work training, professional development, and career advancement,

- These are intended to be good starting points, not a comprehensive catalogue.

We welcome constructive feedback to improve this page and these lists.

- GitHub issues and/or pull requests are welcome.

- The resources are found via AI searches then go through human-in-the-loop proofing.

## Blog posts

- [Product Roadmap 2025 Guide with Examples & Templates](https://productschool.com/blog/product-strategy/what-is-a-product-roadmap): An agile product roadmap is a flexible, iterative plan that outlines the strategic direction and prioritized work for a product, allowing for frequent adjustments based on feedback, changing priorities, and ongoing learning. The guide emphasizes adapting based on stakeholder feedback, market changes, and performance data.

- [Tips for Agile Product Roadmaps & Product Roadmap Examples](https://www.scrum.org/resources/blog/tips-agile-product-roadmaps-product-roadmap-examples): From Scrum.org, this blog explains that a product roadmap is a high-level, strategic plan that describes the likely development of the product over the next period of time, supporting the product's purpose and vision while helping Product Owners keep their stakeholders aligned.

- [2025 Product Management Trends: Shape the Future](https://shorterloop.com/the-product-mindset/posts/product-management-trends-transforming-the-future-two-decades/): Published January 2025, this blog emphasizes that Agile allows teams to pivot seamlessly—adjusting priorities, refining the roadmap, and delivering updates that keep you competitive. The ability to understand and connect with users, stakeholders, and your team is essential.

- [Product OKRs: Driving Outcomes Over Outputs](https://productschool.com/blog/product-strategy/product-okrs): OKRs provide product managers with a powerful framework to align teams, track progress, and influence without direct authority, by focusing on outcomes rather than outputs to clearly communicate strategic goals. Emphasizes avoiding vanity metrics and setting achievable goals.

- [OKRs for Product Managers: Practical Guide & Examples 2025](https://www.thrivesparrow.com/blog/okrs-for-product-managers): Objectives are qualitative, inspirational goals that provide direction and define success, while Key Results are quantifiable, time-bound metrics that measure progress. Common mistakes to avoid include focusing on outputs rather than outcomes, having too many OKRs, and misalignment with company strategy.

- [Product Lifecycle Management: Best Practices for Faster Time to Market](https://www.etq.com/blog/product-lifecycle-management-best-practices-for-faster-time-to-market/): Best practices for PLM implementation include setting clear objectives, involving cross-functional teams, choosing the right software, comprehensive training, data consistency, and continuous improvement.

- [Product Discovery Framework For Digital Product Companies](https://adaptmethodology.com/blog/product-discovery-framework/): Product discovery is the process of deeply understanding user problems, needs, and opportunities before building a product or feature. It involves research, validation, and experimentation to ensure that what a team builds will solve a real problem and deliver meaningful value.

- [Continuous Product Discovery](https://www.producttalk.org/): Teresa Torres' Product Talk blog defines continuous discovery as "weekly touch points with customers, by the team building the products, where they conduct small research activities, in pursuit of a desired product outcome." Her framework has helped thousands of product teams integrate customer feedback into daily decision-making.

- [Prioritization Frameworks for Product Managers: RICE, MoSCoW, and Beyond](https://arbisoft.com/blogs/prioritization-frameworks-for-product-managers-rice-mo-s-co-w-and-beyond): This comprehensive post covers five proven frameworks—Value vs. Effort, MoSCoW, RICE, Kano Model, and Opportunity Scoring. It explores why prioritization is important and shares actionable ways to tackle it effectively.

- [Product Management KPIs: 15+ Metrics to Track](https://www.atlassian.com/agile/product-management/product-management-kpis): Product management KPIs are metrics that quantify the success of a product and guide every strategic decision. The post covers creating dedicated KPI dashboards with customizable charts, graphs, or tables with team member and stakeholder access.

## Research articles

- [A Comprehensive Overview of Software Product Management Challenges](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s10664-022-10134-5): This academic paper examines the challenges software product managers face, including gathering data, requirements, and accessing customers to develop valuable solutions. Determining the true value of the product that customers need represents the essence of software product management. Empirical Software Engineering.

- [Agile Management in Product Development](https://www.tandfonline.com/doi/full/10.1080/08956308.2019.1638488): This article examines agile's emergence in software development in the 1990s and discusses agile values including collaboration, team empowerment, iterative and incremental development, increased customer engagement, and adaptability to change. Research-Technology Management (Vol. 62, No. 5, 2019).

- [Agile Methodologies for Improved Product Management](https://carijournals.org/journals/index.php/JBSM/article/view/1439): This research investigates the adoption of agile methodologies in product management and assesses their impact on product development processes, organizational culture, and overall business performance. The study found benefits including increased product innovation, faster time-to-market, improved customer satisfaction, and enhanced adaptability. Journal of Business and Strategic Management.

- [Toward a Comprehensive Framework of Value Proposition Development](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0019850119309940): The customer value proposition (CVP) is an important concept integrating theory and practice. This paper presents an integrative framework with three components: dimensions (strategic vs. operational), perspectives (customer, company, co-creation, context), and processes (identifying competitive CVPs, developing competencies, facilitating value creation, and setting goals). ScienceDirect.

- [How to Prioritize Your Product Roadmap: A Grey Literature Review](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/351587529_How_to_Prioritize_Your_Product_Roadmap_When_Everything_Feels_Important_A_Grey_Literature_Review): This grey literature review categorized 170 articles into five subject areas including product roadmap prioritization techniques. The study identified 18 techniques for prioritizing product roadmap content, with the most frequently mentioned being the Impact/Effort matrix, Kano Model, and MoSCoW method. ResearchGate.

- [Stakeholder Engagement: Past, Present, and Future](https://journals.sagepub.com/doi/full/10.1177/00076503211066595): This literature review analyzes 90 articles in leading academic journals focusing on stakeholder engagement in business and society, management and strategy, and environmental management. The research identifies that co-creation and co-production activities represent a form of high-quality collaborative relationship. SAGE Journals (2022). Johanna Kujala, Sybille Sachs, Heta Leinonen, Anna Heikkinen, Daniel Laude.

- [Stakeholder Diversity and Collaborative Innovation](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/pii/S0148296323003132): This paper uses Stakeholder Theory to assess how a choice of stakeholders with diverse interests affects collaborative innovation. Results show that collaborations with secondary stakeholders are associated with eco-innovations, while primary stakeholders are associated with product and process innovations. ScienceDirect (2023).

- [Stakeholder Engagement in Co-creation Processes for Innovation](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0148296319305855): This systematic literature review addresses stakeholder engagement, open innovation, and co-creation. It emphasizes that stakeholders must become engaged in actively cooperating with the firm to plan, develop, co-create or improve products/brands. ScienceDirect (2019).

- [Customer Value Anticipation, Product Innovativeness, and Customer Lifetime Value](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0148296316300923): This study examines the relationship among customer value anticipation, product innovativeness and customer lifetime value (CLV), defining customer value anticipation as the firm's ability to sense, foresee, and anticipate what specific customers will value from its offerings. ScienceDirect.

- [Customer Involvement in Innovation: A Review of Literature](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/325721477_Customer_Involvement_in_Innovation_A_Review_of_Literature_and_Future_Research_Directions): Research emphasizes that knowing the requirements of customers is one of the most critical factors for innovation, and understanding customer needs and market awareness is a consistent theme for the success of product development. ResearchGate.

## Books

- [Inspired: How to Create Tech Products Customers Love](https://www.amazon.com/INSPIRED-Create-Tech-Products-Customers/dp/1119387507): Written by Marty Cagan, founder of Silicon Valley Product Group (SVPG), this book is the primary reference for technology product managers. It focuses on how top companies like Google, Netflix, and Amazon create great products people love. The first edition, published ten years ago, established itself as essential reading for PMs. Marty Cagan.

- [Empowered: Ordinary People, Extraordinary Products](https://www.amazon.com/EMPOWERED-Ordinary-Extraordinary-Products-Silicon/dp/111969129X): Marty Cagan's follow-up to "Inspired," focusing specifically on product leadership and how to build and lead strong product teams. This book unlocks your team's full potential and is ideal for experienced product professionals ready to step into more strategic roles. Marty Cagan.

- [Escaping the Build Trap: How Effective Product Management Creates Real Value](https://www.amazon.com/Escaping-Build-Trap-Effective-Management/dp/149197379X): This book highlights why many product teams fall into the habit of just building more, rather than solving real problems. It's essential for shifting focus from outputs to outcomes and teaches how to lay the foundation for great product management that helps companies solve real customer problems while achieving business goals. Melissa Perri.

- [Continuous Discovery Habits: Discover Products that Create Customer Value and Business Value](https://www.amazon.com/Continuous-Discovery-Habits-Discover-Products/dp/1736633309): Teresa Torres is an internationally acclaimed author, speaker, and coach who teaches a structured and sustainable approach to continuous discovery. This book, which has sold more than 80,000 copies worldwide, helps product teams infuse their daily product decisions with customer input through weekly touch points and small research activities. Teresa Torres.

- [The Lean Product Playbook: How to Innovate with Minimum Viable Products](https://www.amazon.com/Lean-Product-Playbook-Innovate-Products/dp/1118960874): This hands-on guide for creating products that resonate with customers offers clear, step-by-step instructions for both startups and large companies using Lean Startup principles. Dan Olsen, a renowned product management trainer, provides practical advice on building winning products. Dan Olsen.

- [Hooked: How to Build Habit-Forming Products](https://www.amazon.com/Hooked-How-Build-Habit-Forming-Products/dp/1591847788): For product managers, designers, marketers, and startup founders who seek to understand how products influence behavior. Nir Eyal provides a compelling framework for understanding user psychology and creating products that are habit-forming. Teaches a four-step process (Trigger, Action, Variable Reward, Investment) that companies like Instagram, Twitter, and Pinterest use. Nir Eyal.

- [The Mom Test: How to Talk to Customers & Learn if Your Business is a Good Idea](https://www.amazon.com/Mom-Test-customers-business-everyone/dp/1492180742): This highly-rated book (4.37/5 from 13,142 ratings) teaches a set of simple rules to ask and frame good questions that even your mom can't lie to you about. It helps product managers navigate the complexities of customer feedback by learning how to frame questions that avoid bias and extract valuable, actionable insights. Rob Fitzpatrick.

- [Cracking the PM Interview: How to Land a Product Manager Job in Technology](https://www.amazon.com/Cracking-PM-Interview-Product-Technology/dp/0984782818): A comprehensive book about landing a product management role in a startup or bigger tech company. Covers various aspects of PM interviews including product questions, behavioral questions, estimation problems, and how to build a strong resume for PM roles. Gayle Laakmann McDowell and Jackie Bavaro.

- [Product Leadership: How Top Product Managers Launch Awesome Products and Build Successful Teams](https://www.amazon.com/Product-Leadership-Managers-Products-Successful/dp/1491960604): Features interviews with nearly 100 leading product managers worldwide, drawing on decades of experience in product design and development. The book is divided into three parts covering building and leading teams, guiding product development through various company growth stages, and collaborating with customers and stakeholders. Richard Banfield, Martin Eriksson, and Nate Walkingshaw.

- [Strategize: Product Strategy and Product Roadmap Practices for the Digital Age](https://www.amazon.com/Strategize-Product-Strategy-Roadmap-Practices/dp/0993499201): A must-read for product managers with an excellent framework for creating and executing product strategy. Roman Pichler provides proven techniques and tools to create effective strategies and actionable roadmaps for digital products. Roman Pichler.

## Professional development tips

- Commit to continuous learning because tools and techniques evolve rapidly
- Build a portfolio of demonstration projects so you can share your skills
- Join relevant communities, attend events, and connect with colleagues
